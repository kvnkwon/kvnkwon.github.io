What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Margin versus Padding

Margin defines the space between the border and other outer elements (The gray area in the photo).

Padding defines space between the border and element 'content' as seen in the photo (The yellow area in the photo).

When do you use margin? Does the elements on your site need space between them? Margin will give you what you need. You will only need to use padding if you have an inner element that needs space between the parent box and itself.

Border

The above graphic further illustrates the areas that padding and margin modify. We can see that the yellow area is the border. If you put a margin of lets say 10px to this box, it will add 10px of space between the border and other surrounding elements on your webpage. If you add a padding of 10px, it will add 10px of space between your border and inner content (Hello World in the photo). Simple right?
