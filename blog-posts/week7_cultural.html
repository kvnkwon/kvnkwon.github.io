A look into myself..

Ok so.. DBC gave us a list of values that we're supposed to choose and write about. The values we choose should be the ones that came to mind when I was happiest or most satisfied in my life. From that long list of words, I immediately chose three: Accomplishment, Confidence, and Recognition. So why those three? Before entering the path to become a super cool developer, I had other dreams.I wanted to be a filmmaker and I was happiest when I was out shooting movies with my friends. I remember in High School, I submitted a zombie film for the film festival and won. It was at that moment, I realized that I had made big steps in chasing my dream which led to a huge feeling of accomplishment. I was recognized by my peers and my parents, who did not completely support this dream until they saw what I could do. I was going to film school in the Fall and I was more confident than ever.

So DBC then asked me to rate on a 1-5 scale of how much I try to live up to these values.

  • Accomplishment: 5
  • Confidence: 5
  • Recognition: 5

Ok. After rating them, I realized that most people would have given them the same rating. I mean, who else wouldn't try to feel accomplished, confident, and be recognized? I think DBC was trying to get me to rate the other values on the list that would make more sense with these ratings. Oh well.

What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on? Another question by DBC. Hmm. A friend of mine is struggling with his career path. He does not want to pursue his nursing studies any longer, and he asks if he should go to a coding bootcamp to become a developer. Ha! I honestly could not really give him the best advice. After all, I'm in his shoes right now as well except that I made the decision to go for it. I have to see my outcome to be able to give anyone advice, but I told him, if he can imagine himself twenty years down the road working on a computer looking at magical text called code on a daily basis- he should be good. Good advice? Meh.
